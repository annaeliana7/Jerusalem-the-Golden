Jerusalem the Golden

Jerusalem the golden, with milk and honey blest— the sight of it refreshes the weary and oppressed.

I know not, oh, I know not what joys await us there, what radiancy of glory, what bliss beyond compare: to sing the hymn unending with all the martyr throng, amidst the halls of Zion resounding full with song.



O sweet and blessed country, the home of God’s elect!

O sweet and blessed country that eager hearts expect, where they who with their leader have conquered in the fight forever and forever are clad in robes of white: in mercy, Jesus, bring us to that dear land of rest where sings the host of heaven your glorious name to bless.



This hymn is a joyful celebration of the city of Jerusalem, described as a place of milk and honey, refreshment for the weary, and a land of great joy and radiancy. The lyrics speak of the hope of one day joining the martyrs in singing this hymn in the halls of Zion, surrounded by the radiance and glory of God.

But "Jerusalem the Golden" is not just a song about a physical place. It is also a song about the eternal rest and bliss that awaits us in heaven, as described in the second verse: "O sweet and blessed country, the home of God's elect! O sweet and blessed country that eager hearts expect, where they who with their leader have conquered in the fight forever and forever are clad in robes of white: in mercy, Jesus, bring us to that dear land of rest where sings the host of heaven your glorious name to bless."

These lyrics remind us that our ultimate destination is not a physical place on earth, but a spiritual one in heaven, where we will be able to join the ranks of those who have conquered in the fight and be clothed in robes of white.

We hope that this hymn brings you comfort and inspiration in your own journey of faith. May the words of "Jerusalem the Golden" remind you of the joy and radiancy that await us in heaven, and may they inspire you to keep fighting the good fight and following Jesus.
